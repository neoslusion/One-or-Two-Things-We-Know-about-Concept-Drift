\chapter*{ABSTRACT}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ABSTRACT}

Currently, concept drift is one of the major challenges that degrades the performance of machine learning systems, especially in continuously changing streaming data environments. This thesis focuses on unsupervised drift detection and classification, with two main contributions: (1) integrating \textbf{IDW-MMD} (Inverse Density-Weighted MMD) into ShapeDD as the validation step of a hybrid detector (Standard MMD trace + IDW with Gamma-approximation null), achieving a 16.5$\times$ throughput improvement over the original permutation-based method (from $\sim$8{,}000 to $\sim$131{,}000 batch samples/s) while maintaining a properly calibrated test (empirical Type-I error 0.04--0.10, within the 95\% CI of the nominal $\alpha = 0.05$) and a competitive F1-score of 0.454 on the 14-dataset detection benchmark with $\delta = 75$ tolerance; and (2) proposing \textbf{SE-CDT} (ShapeDD-Enhanced Concept Drift Type), an unsupervised drift type classification method based on MMD signal shape analysis, achieving 82.2\% category accuracy (TCD vs PCD) and 65.8\% micro-averaged / 55.7\% macro-averaged subcategory accuracy across five drift types, augmented with a \textit{Concept Memory} module that pushes Recurrent-drift identification from 33.1\% to 91.5\% and an \textit{online self-calibration} mechanism that adaptively adjusts classification thresholds at runtime. Additionally, the thesis implements a \textbf{Type-Specific Adaptation} framework that selects appropriate model update strategies based on the classified drift type, improving accuracy by +16.57pp on Stepping Drift and +17.10pp on Sudden Drift over no adaptation, with $N = 30$ runs and Friedman test confirming significance ($p < 10^{-15}$ on all three scenarios). The system is designed on an Apache Kafka architecture for real-time streaming data processing.
